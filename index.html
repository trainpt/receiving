Field App

Create Production Records & Field Tickets

Used by crews and foremen in the field to log harvested units and group them into scannable field tickets before the load is built.

Setup

Should you use field tickets?

The receiving product leans on production records within PickTrace. If those records have a unique ID, receiving can reference them directly. If not, field tickets bridge the gap.

Do production records have a unique ID (tag, label, or paired label)?
No
Enable field tickets. Without a unique ID there is nothing for receiving to scan. Field tickets group records together so they become trackable.
Yes
Move to the next question below.
Does the grower need to know which specific unit is on which pallet?
Yes
Enable field tickets. Example: strawberry growers building pallets in the field.
No
Not required. Pallets are created by scanning a single reference production unit's unique ID.
Field tickets are independent of receiving. They can be enabled for any customer at any time, even if they are not using receiving. Managed at the work bundle level.
Field Tickets

Digitize your harvest release forms

Start here. The Field App is where you create the actual production records that the Receiving App will use. Most growers already use paper field tickets — the process is the same, just with a scannable barcode added.

Use any barcode generator to create your field ticket barcodes before heading to the field. The barcodes must follow this format — we provide the format, not the barcode itself.

#FT032501
#FT followed by an alphanumeric string (e.g., date + sequence number). This format is mandatory — no exceptions.
  • 1
    Enable field ticket tracking in the work bundle (Production tab).
  • 2
    The app requires a field ticket to be selected before any scanning. A dropdown and + button appear at the top of the screen.
  • 3
    Scan the pre-printed FT barcode, then scan employee badges to credit units (totes, bins) to that ticket.
    For some table grape growers, the field ticket represents a trailer or a pallet on the trailer.
  • 4
    To switch tickets, scan a new FT barcode. Any unsaved record in the queue moves to the new ticket automatically.
    Records that have already been saved stay on the original field ticket.
  • 5
    Optionally set a max field ticket count on the pack style (e.g., 56 totes per field ticket). The cap is determined by the first unit scanned onto the ticket.
  • 6
    Sync data to the cloud before switching to the Receiving App.
Pre-print your barcodes. Creating tickets manually in the app generates long offline-compatible codes that are harder to manage. Generate and print them ahead of time.
Receiving App

Build & Track Loads from the Field

Used by third-party drivers to create load tickets, scan field tickets, and share load summaries with the receiving team at the destination.

Load Creation

Build a load in under a minute

The Receiving App is used by third-party drivers to build load tickets and scan field tickets. In most cases, the driver — not the crew — is the primary user.

  • 1
    Open the Receiving App and tap New to create a load ticket.
  • 2
    Complete the logistics screen: destination site, current site/location (where fruit is being picked up), driver name, company, and trailer ID.
    All fields are optional and take under 30 seconds. Driver, company, and trailer ID are typed in as free text. After the first load, these fields auto-populate — the driver only fills them once.
  • 3
    Tap Save and Continue, then scan your FT barcodes. Associated units appear on the load instantly — even if the device is offline.
  • 4
    Tap Finish when all field tickets have been scanned.
User-based login. The Receiving App requires user credentials (email/password), not an employee badge. It is a separate app from the PT Field App, available on the App Store and Play Store. A user account must be created for every person who needs access. The destination list can be limited by the user's role permissions.
Scanning

Scan field tickets onto the load

After completing logistics, scan the FT barcodes created in the field app. Each scan pulls in all associated units — crew, block, variety, and tote count — instantly.

  • 1
    Tap + Add or use the scanner to add field tickets to the load.
  • 2
    Associated units appear on the load showing crew, block, and count per field ticket.
  • 3
    Data is cached from the field app sync — this works even if the device is offline.
Device Offline? The app will show a loading indicator to the right of the Field Ticket ID — just wait for it to finish loading before proceeding.
Reference ID

Scanning without field tickets

For table grape growers, units aren't grouped onto a field ticket. Instead, scan any single unit on the pallet — the app will ask how many are just like it.

  • 1
    Scan any one unit on the pallet as a reference ID.
  • 2
    Enter the total number of units on that pallet (e.g., 80). The app finds that ID in the back end and backfills all associated production data.
  • 3
    Repeat pallet by pallet. Each pallet gets its own scan — the ID of the unit you scanned becomes the pallet ID so it can always be traced back.
Scan pallet by pallet — not the whole truck at once. A truckload is rarely all alike. It may have different blocks, pack styles, or varieties across pallets. Scanning each pallet separately keeps those splits accurate. If you scan the entire truck as one entry, it all gets attributed to a single unit type from a single block, which will throw off receiving.

You lose granularity with this method — you won't know which specific boxes are on which pallet. What you get is total pallet count and total box count. That's the trade-off for growers who don't track units to pallets.

Load Summary

Share via QR code or PDF

Once the load is built, long press it to pull up the QR code. It can be texted, shown on screen, or downloaded as a PDF.

  • 1
    Long press the load and select QR Code or Download PDF.
  • 2
    The PDF shows all logistics at the top — driver details, employer of the fruit, crews represented, and destination.
    Below that: the total number of field tickets (pallets). When field tickets are the same, they're grouped together. When there's a variation, they're broken out separately. Field ticket IDs are listed.
  • 3
    At the destination, the driver shows the QR code to the receiver. If they can't present it, receiving devices automatically show all loads destined for that location — no QR code needed.
    Driver view: device-oriented — only sees loads they created or scanned.
    Receiver view: destination scoping — sees every load headed to their destination. This is set on the office side via permissions.
  • 4
    99% of the time, both drivers and receivers will live on the Loads page. If you need to look at a specific pallet, tap the Field Tickets tab to see every field ticket on the load — but most people won't need to go there often.
Change status before leaving. Drivers must manually change the load status from Loading to In Transit when they leave the field — this starts the transit timer in the office tab.
At the Destination

What the receiver sees

When a driver arrives, the goal is to show the QR code. The receiver scans it and the load pulls up instantly.

If the driver can't present the QR code, the receiving device shows every load headed to that destination automatically — no action needed from the driver. This is controlled by destination scoping set in the office.

Driver vs. receiver view. Drivers only see loads they created or came into contact with. Receivers see all loads destined for their location. Two different permissions, two different views of the same data.
Unit Search

Trace any unit back to the field

Scan any unit with a unique ID — bin, table grape box, strawberry box — to pull up its full production record: the employee who picked it, the crew, pick time, and any notes.

If receiving counted 60 but the production record shows 50, the discrepancy is immediately visible. Notes added here stay attached to the record and appear the next time the unit is scanned.

Flagging issues. If damage or a discrepancy is found, add a note from either the office tab or the mobile app. A red exclamation mark appears on the load record, visible to anyone with access.
Discrepancy

Catch mismatches before they reach inventory

The discrepancy page identifies mismatches between what was sent from the field and what was received. This feeds directly into sellable inventory counts — errors have immediate downstream effects.

Inventory impact. The receiving process feeds directly into what the grower can sell. Do not disrupt this process — fruit needs to reach the cooler as fast as possible.
  • 1
    Confirm how many crews the driver is collecting from before pickup.
  • 2
    Ensure only one receiving process per driver at a time. Multiple simultaneous processes prevent proper reconciliation.
  • 3
    For some table grape growers, each tractor driver handles two crews within a block — a consistent, trackable setup.
Work in progress. The discrepancy page is actively being developed. The core goal — surfacing mismatches at the field ticket level — is in place, but the full workflow is not yet finalized.
